String Plucking Impulse UGen built on SinOsc drone
Sebastian James

For part 1, I played around with the FM synthesis examples. I chose to manipulate the "Chuck-time" variable, changing the dac output from every sample, to every 1000 samples. I noticed that as I increased the time between samples being sent to dac, the "wave-like" appearance of the carrier signal modulating became more apparent. At this sample rate I noticed the most desirable sound, changing what once appeared as a constant tone, to a noticeable oscillating output.

For part 2, I first used the SinOsc UGen to generate an A major third drone at 440 Hz. I then built a string "pluck" sound using an impulse UGen to play over this drone at random frequencies. Furthermore, I then hard coded an array of notes in the A-major triad (using their respective MIDI note values) to only play consonant sounds. I initially ran into issues writing the delay for the string plucks. I either was overloaded with feedback, or recieved a short tone. It taught me alot playing around with the Impulse UGen and how it can be manipulated vastly with only small increments in its associated values. The other issue I noticed was that if I played the plucks at lower frequencies, the delay filter was overloaded and the sound would be cut and an error message would appear in the console. I fixed this by raising the plucks by one octave.
